# Long, complex tickets — particularly German-language emails describing multiple problems — take five to six minutes just to triage before any actual customer help begins, consuming disproportionate agent capacity.

> **Composite score** 4.13 / 5  · **Rubric** v1.0

## The Problem

Tier 1 agents spend five to six minutes triaging complex, multi-issue tickets before any customer help begins — a disproportionate capacity drain at 2,100 tickets per week, 60% in German. As Sebastian describes it, a "long German email from an HR generalist who's described three problems in one message" forces agents to manually parse intent, cross-reference the customer record, and assign category and priority before touching the actual issue. A 26% miscategorisation rate compounds the cost, routing tickets to the wrong team and triggering rework.

## The Solution

An AI layer reads each incoming ticket, identifies distinct issues within a single message, and outputs a structured summary — suggested category, priority signal, and a plain-language problem statement — before the agent opens the ticket. For German and other non-English tickets, the system works across languages without requiring translation. Agents skip the reading-and-bucketing phase and start solving. Triage time on complex tickets drops from five-plus minutes toward the 90-second baseline seen on clean tickets today.

## Feasibility & Effort

Zendesk is already the intake layer, and the nine-category taxonomy plus priority schema are well-defined — good training signal. The riskiest dependency is category ground truth: with a 26% existing miscategorisation rate, labelled data for fine-tuning or prompt validation needs a manual audit pass first. Multilingual handling is table-stakes for modern summarisation systems but must be validated against German HR-specific terminology. Realistic build is a quarter — two to three weeks of data prep, four to six weeks of integration and agent-facing UI, plus a calibration period.
