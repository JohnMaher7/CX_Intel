# Support tickets that signal competitive evaluation (e.g. asking whether the product does something a competitor does) are not flagged or routed, so they are lost in the Zendesk queue without triggering any action.

> **Composite score** 3.47 / 5  · **Rubric** v1.0

## The Problem

Competitive-intent support tickets are invisible to the renewals and CS teams who need them most. When customers submit tickets asking "does your tool do X?" — where X is a competitor's strength — those tickets sit in the Zendesk queue with no flag, no routing, and no downstream action. Lin confirmed that in post-mortems on zero-notice churns, signals like these were present in the data "we just didn't see them." CSMs like Bibi are left doing manual archaeology across Zendesk, Gong, and Mixpanel to reconstruct risk that was already written down.

## The Solution

A text classifier reads incoming Zendesk tickets and identifies language that signals competitive comparison or feature-gap frustration. Flagged tickets are tagged and routed — to the owning CSM, to a Slack alert, or to a Salesforce field — so the signal reaches the right person within hours, not after a post-mortem. The CSM's day changes concretely: instead of discovering a competitive threat on a renewal call, they see a tagged ticket in their queue with context to act on while the account is still winnable.

## Feasibility & Effort

Zendesk is already the system of record, so ticket ingestion is straightforward. The classifier itself is a well-understood task — labeled examples of competitive-intent tickets are the main input needed, and a small human-reviewed training set from past churns could be assembled quickly. The riskiest dependency is routing: Salesforce and Slack integrations require coordination with Sales Ops and IT. Realistically this is a six-to-eight week build end-to-end — two weeks for the classifier, the remainder for integrations, alerting logic, and CSM-facing UX.
