# Executive brief — Sales → Implementation Consultant Handoff — Discovery Session

> Synthesised from 3 AI-solvable opportunities, ranked by composite score.

## Where the team is bleeding value

This discovery session examined the Sales-to-Implementation Consultant handoff — specifically the chain of failures between a deal closing in Salesforce and an IC walking into a prepared kickoff call. The team is bleeding time and customer trust at three distinct points: a structurally broken Implementation Brief, hours of manual artefact archaeology, and critical requirements surfacing too late. As Lena put it, "about 25% of the time I get on the kickoff call and the customer says something like, 'Right, so we specifically bought this because we need it to integrate with our existing Slack' — and I'm hearing it for the first time." Rachel's data ties these failures directly to a 2.1x first-year churn multiplier for projects slipping past go-live by more than 30 days. AI applied across these three failure points could convert the company's existing Gong, Drive, and Notion artefacts into reliable, structured handoff intelligence before the kickoff clock even starts.

## The three biggest opportunities

### 1. Eliminate surprise requirements on kickoff calls by mining Gong before handoff · composite 4.27

This is the highest-leverage intervention because it attacks the root cause of the 6.5-week average go-live slip. One in four kickoffs is contaminated by requirements the AE heard but never forwarded — requirements that are already captured in six to twelve Gong transcripts per deal. Closing that gap directly targets the churn multiplier Rachel quantified in November.

### 2. Auto-generate the Implementation Brief from existing deal artefacts to fix a 62% failure rate · composite 4.08

The 22-field Brief has a 38% completion rate, and half of those are unusable — meaning ICs start roughly four in five projects without reliable structured context. An AI draft pulling employee count, payroll provider, integrations, and go-live date from Gong, Outlook, and the SE scoping deck removes the dependency on AE discipline without requiring a process change AEs will resist.

### 3. Cut IC onboarding time by synthesising scattered deal artefacts into a single structured summary · composite 3.72

Lena spends three to four hours per new customer reconstructing context from Gong, Outlook, Google Drive, and Notion — consuming roughly half of the 5-business-day kickoff SLA while managing 11 active implementations. For Sara, six weeks in with no institutional memory, that cost is even higher. A multi-source deal summary directly reclaims capacity the team cannot currently hire its way out of at €85k per head.

## Recommended next step

Pilot the Gong requirements-flagging solution on the next ten Closed Won deals before kickoff. Validated means: an IC confirms the AI-surfaced flag list matches or exceeds what they would have found manually, and at least one previously missed requirement is caught per deal on average.
