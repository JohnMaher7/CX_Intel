# Executive brief — Tier 1 Support Triage — Discovery Session

> Synthesised from 3 AI-solvable opportunities, ranked by composite score.

## Where the team is bleeding value

This discovery session mapped the full Tier 1 support lifecycle inside Zendesk — from initial triage through categorisation, macro-driven resolution, and escalation to Tier 2 — across roughly 2,100 tickets per week in six languages. The team is bleeding capacity at every handoff: complex multilingual tickets consume five to six minutes before any customer help begins, a quarter of tickets land in the wrong queue, and nearly a third of escalations bounce back for missing context. As Priya put it, when Product asks what's blowing up, "we answer from gut feel." AI applied across triage, classification, and escalation drafting could convert that gut-feel operation into a measurable, self-correcting support system.

## The three biggest opportunities

### 1. Slash pre-help triage time on complex multilingual tickets with LLM summarisation · composite 4.13

At 2,100 tickets per week with 60% arriving in German, even a modest share of multi-issue emails hitting the five-to-six-minute triage ceiling represents a significant weekly capacity drain. Structured decomposition by an LLM would let agents begin solving immediately rather than spending the first third of their time just parsing the problem.

### 2. Eliminate the DATEV-versus-payroll misrouting that silently breaks four-hour SLAs · composite 4.05

Twenty-six percent miscategorisation — concentrated on DATEV integration errors tagged as payroll — directly triggers the four-hour SLA clock on the wrong team. An LLM classifier distinguishing symptom language like 'DATEV export failed' from genuine payroll-rules issues would protect SLA compliance and reduce cross-team rerouting without any agent behaviour change.

### 3. Auto-draft escalation summaries to stop Tier 2 bouncing a third of handoffs · composite 3.93

A 32% bounce-back rate on escalations adds a full customer round-trip while the first-response SLA is already running. Because agents skip the free-text 'what I've tried' field under time pressure, Tier 2 consistently lacks repro context — a gap an LLM pulling from the ticket thread and conversation history could close automatically.

## Recommended next step

Pilot the LLM ticket summariser on the DE queue first: pull two weeks of five-plus-minute triage tickets from Zendesk, run them through a summarisation prototype, and validate with Sebastian that structured output reduces agent read time below two minutes on at least 80% of sampled cases.
